Step-By-Step Guide To Becoming A Game Designer

Your roadmap from gaming enthusiast to becoming a professional designer
1

Discover Your Passion

Explore different types of games and find what excites you. Puzzle games? RPGs? Indie art games? Know what you love—it shapes what you create.

  • Play at least 3 type of different game genres each week.
  • Identify what mechanics you enjoy the most.
  • Join gaming communities to discuss your favourites and collect other's opinion about it.
2

Learn the Basics

Understand the fundamentals of game design.

  • Game Mechanics - Rules and systems that define gameplay
  • Level Design - Creating engaging environments and progression
  • User Experience (UX) - How players interact with your game
  • Storytelling In Games - Narrative techniwues for immersion

You also can find free resources on Youtube, Blogs and beginner courses.

3

Play Critically

Don't just play on analyzing. Sometimes asking yourself questions also can be helpful too.

  • Why is this fun?
  • What work and what doesn't?
  • How could I improve this game?

This builds your design instincts. Keep a game journal to record your observations.

4

Try Making Some Simple Games

Use those beginner-friendly tools to create your very first games!

  • Scratch (great for first-timers)
  • Twine (interactive fiction)
  • Construct or Bitsy (visual tools, no coding needed)

Start small. Even a one-minute game is valuable. Your first 10 games will teach you more than any course.

5

Learn A Game Engine

When you are ready to surf more deeper, lets try some professional game engines!

  • Unity (C# coding, 2D & 3D)
  • Unreal Engine (blueprints & C++, 3D focus)
  • Godot (lightweight and open-source)

Choose those game engine according to your interest and also your project goals. But! Focus on one engine on your first year.

6

Build A Portfolio

Create and share your games—on itch.io, Game Jams, or your own site. Document your process. Show your creativity and growth.

  • Include 3-5 of your best projects
  • Showcase about your different skills (mechanics, UI, storytelling and etc)
  • Provide documentation of your design process
  • Include post-mortens for completed projects
7

Collaborate And Get Feedback

Join online communities (Discord, Reddit, etc.) or school clubs. Talk with artists, writers, coders—game design is teamwork.

  • Participate in game jams (Ludum Dare, Glob al Game Jam and etc)
  • Join Discord servers for game developers
  • Attend local meetups or conferences
  • Find mentors in the industry
8

Keep Growing

Game design is always evolving. Keep learning from new games, new tech, and other designers. Stay curious. Experiment a lot.

  • Follow industry news and trends
  • Learn more complementary skills (art, programming, sound effects and etc)
  • Revisit and improve your old projects
